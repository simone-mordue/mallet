In social protection, Guinea faces two main challenges. First, it is necessary to extend the currently existing formal system to the
beneficiaries of non-contributory schemes while implementing the necessary reforms to make it more efficient and sustainable
system. The second  challenge  is to ensure that the new social protection policy is operational, inclusive, efficient and sustainable
for the poor and vulnerable, to reduce the social risks to which people are exposed, to mitigate the potential consequences of the
advent of these risks, and to help households who suffer them to handle it without major damage. Currently, Guinea, a very large
number of people below the poverty line and belonging to vulnerable groups do not have access to social protection and have a
permanent lack of access to basic social services without support and a state management. The rural and informal sector are
virtually excluded from social protection as they focus the vast majority of assets
